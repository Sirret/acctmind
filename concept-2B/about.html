About ACCTS

My story is why ACCTS exists

Naturopathic nutritionist. Mother. Ten years of walking with families through the hardest seasons of their lives.

★★★★★ 10+ years · 36 countries · 1000s of families

My work did not begin in a clinic. It began in my own home.

Years ago, my father suffered a severe stroke. I stayed by his side through his recovery, watching him fight his way back to health — a fight he won so completely that he went on to complete a degree in theology. Walking with him through that season changed how I understood the brain, the body, and what it truly means to heal.

Not long after, I found myself walking a different road with my own son, who was diagnosed with severe autism, epilepsy, and learning difficulties. I poured everything I had — every ounce of knowledge, faith, and determination — into supporting his recovery. Today, he is thirteen years old, has overcome each of those diagnoses, and remains free of medication for epilepsy — living evidence that the brain is able to heal when it is properly supported.

I did all of this as a single mother, building a business, raising a child with complex needs, and holding my own life together through seasons that tested me in every way. That lived experience — not just clinical training — is the foundation of everything I now do for the families I work with.

Over the past ten years, that personal journey has grown into a life's work supporting families across 36 countries. ACCTS exists because I know, from the inside, what it costs a family to keep going when a child's brain is not working the way it should — and what is possible when it is finally, properly supported.

Changing outcomes, one brain at a time.
